Here I discuss the differences between text based links and buttons.

Creating text based links is a simple process that can be done in dreamweaver as the program itself does most of the work for you. To create a text based link, all one has to do is select a word, phrase, or image and drag the little target arrow in dreamweaver to the file the chosen selection is supposed to be linked to, for external links and e-mails, one must simply type in the url in the specified box.

Creating a button however, is much more complicated. In order to create a button, one must create the picture of the buttons in each state and export it from fireworks as an html. That html file is then linked to dreamweaver and each button must then be linked to something in order for it to work. To link a button however is the same as linking text in dreamweaver.

So, a button is basically a multi stage image used in place of text for a text based link.

Each form of link has its own strengths and weaknesses. In this particular case though, the main weaknesses of one form of link are the strengths of the other. Text based links are smaller and thus more can be added, text based links can also be easily incorporated into the textual content of a page, linke a word in a sentence or a phrase in a paragraph. On the down side however, text based links can be boring and potentially difficult to see if embedded into a lot of text, they can also be easily overlooked if all at the top of the page, maybe mistaken for a title. Buttons on the other hand, are easily noticeable, they stand out in and out of text and are hard to be mistaken for anything other than a button, they are also larger and often coloured, and as such aren't as boring as text links. Their size however, is also a weakness as that makes it difficult to fit a lot of buttons on one page, it also makes it difficult to embed a button into text without it breaking the coherency of the paragraph.

Personally, as you can tell from my site, although they are more of a hassle to create and are more time consuming, I prefer the buttons.
